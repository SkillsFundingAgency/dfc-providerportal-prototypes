About this job


Restaurant managers are in charge of a venue. They make sure customers are happy with the food and service they receive.

Ways to become a restaurant manager


You can become a restaurant manager by doing a college course, apprenticeship or graduate scheme.

You could also get a job in a restaurant and then work your way up.


College courses

You can do a level 3 or level 4 college course in hospitality leadership to become a restaurant manager.

To apply for a level 3 course you’ll usually need 4 or 5 GCSEs or equivalent at grades 9 to 4 (A* to C). View the qualifications that are equal to GCSEs.

To apply for a level 4 course, you’ll need 1 or 2 A levels, a level 3 diploma or restaurant work experience.

You might be able to get help with the cost of your course.

Find college courses near you

Apprenticeships

Another way to become a restaurant manager is by doing an advanced or higher apprenticeship called hospitality supervisor or manager.

To do an advanced apprenticeship, you’ll usually need 5 GCSEs or equivalent at grades 9 to 4 (A* to C). You’ll also need some A levels or equivalent to do a higher apprenticeship. View the qualifications that are equal to A levels.

Apprenticeships are paid jobs that combine training on the job with studying. View more about how apprenticeships work.

Find an apprenticeships near you

Graduate schemes

Many large restaurants and fast food chains run graduate schemes that lead to management jobs.

You'll need a degree in any subject to apply for a graduate scheme

Working your way up

You could start working in a restaurant as a waiter, kitchen staff or bartender.

With experience you could then take on more responsibilities and apply for more senior positions.

Skills you need


As a restaurant manager, you’ll need to be able to:


  • serve customers
  • lead, motivate and manage a team
  • work well with others
  • accept criticism
  • work well under pressure
  • use a computer well
  • stay patient and calm in stressful situations

You’ll also need to learn how to manage a business and what different food production methods are.

Certificates and background checks

Before you can be a restaurant manager, you’ll need to get a hygiene certificate and pass an enhanced background check.

Tasks and responsibilities


As a restaurant manager you could:


  • plan menus with your chefs
  • organise shifts and rotas for kitchen and waiting staff
  • manage stock control and budgets
  • work closely with food and drink suppliers
  • recruit new staff and help to train them
  • deal with customer complaints
  • make sure the restaurant follows licensing, food hygiene, and health and safety rules

You may also help plan large events like weddings and birthdays that take place at the restaurant.

Working environment

Your working environment may be physically demanding.

You may also need to wear a uniform.

Future career path

When you have some experience as a restaurant manager, you could:


  • manage a chain of restaurants or hotels
  • cater large events as a catering manager
  • open your own restaurant

Find out more about career paths you could take from the Hospitality Guild.
